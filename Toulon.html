ِِAhmed Ibn Tulun Mosque

History

The mosque was built from the beginning of the year 265 AH to the year 879 by Sultan Ahmed Ibn Tulun, and it is considered the second oldest mosque in the history of Egypt and the only remaining monument in the city of Al-Qata’i, which he took as the capital of the Tulunid state ,The Ibn Tulun Mosque is located in a slightly elevated area on Al-Khudairi Street in the Al-Sayeda Zainab neighborhood

Architecture

The total area of the mosque, including the increases, is 26,318 square meters. It is built of mud bricks. The horizontal plan of the mosque is in the form of a square with a side length of 162 m. It follows the orthogonal style that was followed in the traditional planning of the university mosques in the region. The site of the mosque consists of a central, square, exposed courtyard, the length of which is 92 m, in the middle of which is a place for ablution, as a result of the renovations carried out in 696 AH / 1296 AD by the Mamluk Sultan Hussam al-Din Lajin, who ruled between [696-698], and it contains a courtyard and is surrounded by 4 roofed surfaces with grilles. Of the columns, the mosque is surrounded from the north, south and west by a large wall, leaving between it and the walls of the building exposed mosques called “Al-Ziyadah”. The Ibn Tulun Mosque is considered one of the largest mosques in Egypt
