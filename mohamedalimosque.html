Mohamed Ali Mosque

History

The mosque built on the site of old Mamluk buildings in cairo's citadel Commissioned by Muhammed ALi Pasha between 1830 and 1848. It is situated on the summit of the citadel and this mosque , along with the citadel , is one of the landmarks and tourist attraction. The archtict of the moaque was Yuduf Bushnak from Istanbul and it's model was the Ahmed Mosque at that city. The mosque was built with a centeral dome surrounded by four small and four semicircular domes. the mosque have two elegant cylindrical minarets of Turkish type with two balconies and conical caps are situated in the westren side of the mosque. The external facade are severe and angular and rise about four stroreys until the level of the lead-covered domes. The mihrab on the southeastren wall in the three storeys high and covered with semicircular dome. There are two arcades on the second storey , rising on columns and covered with domes. There are three entrances on each side of the forecourt , usual entry is through the northeastren gate. There is a brass clock tower in the middel of the northwestren riwak , which was presented to Muhammed Ali by king Louis Philip of France in the 1845.

Architecture

Muhammed ALi chose to build his state mosque entirely in the architectural style of his former overlords.
The Ottomans , unlike the Mamluks who , despite their political submission to the Ottomans ,stuck to the architectural styles of the previous Mamluk dynasties.
