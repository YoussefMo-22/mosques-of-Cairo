Al-Azhar Mosque

History

Al-Azhar is today the most celebrated of all the Cairo's medieval mosques , more because of it's historic and religious importance than it's aeshetic value .
It was the first mosque built in Fatimid Cairo and the first theological college , and has played a continuous role in the history of the City from it's foundation to the present day .
The Mosque of Al-Azhar like the mosque of amr , has undergone a series of enlargements and restorations throughout it's history because of it's importance.
today , all styles and all periods of Cairo's history are represented in it's architecture.

The Name of the Mosque

Historians have disagreed on the origin of the naming this Mosque. It is most likely that the Fatimids named it Al-Azhar after Fatima Az-Zahra, daughter of Prophet Muhammed .
Another explanation for the name Al-Azhar is that drived from the nearby Fatimid palaces known collectivelyas al-qusar al-Zahira (the Brilliant palaces)

Architecture

Fatimid Era

Caliph al-Hakim restord the mosque , donating a new wooden door which is now in the cairo islamic museum.
Caliph al-Amir donated a wooden prayer niche , now also at the museum.
Caliph al-Hafiz li-din allah who added an arcade around the court yard to give mosque a four arcade .
Caliph also added a dome on front of the transept.

Ayyubed and Mamluk Era

Salah al-din al-ayyubi elliminated the Fatimid caliphate and reinstated sunni islam as the state religion in Egypt.
He proceeded to ban Friday prayer at al-Azhar and the friday prayer held only in the mosque of al-Hakim.
The Friday prayer restored to al-Azhar mosque at the reign of al-Zhair Baybars.
Two majestic featuers which now dominate the sahn are the minarets of al-Sultan Qi'itbay and al-Ghuri.

The Ottoman Era

Amir abd al-Rahman Katkhuda during eighteenth century raised the floor and extended the prayer hall.
He also work in the facade of the main entrance to the mosque (Bab al-Muzayyinin).
