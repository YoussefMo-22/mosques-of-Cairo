Al-Sultan Hasan Mosque

History

The Sultan Hassan Mosque and Madrasa is one of the largest and most architecturally elaborate mosques in all of Egypt. The founding of this university began in 757 AD. By Aa-Sultan Hasan, the building was still unfinished when he died. One of his chiefs, Bashir Aghaad Jandar, completed his 754 H. It sits on the edge of Muhammad Ali street, opposite his adjacent 19th-century Al-Rifai Mosque in Salah al-Din Square.

Architecture

Sultan Hassan's complex was built between 1356 and 1363 and included a madrasah, a congregational mosque, and a mausoleum. A free-standing complex with a monumental domed mausoleum flanked by only one surviving minaret is prominently located beneath the citadel facing the monumental portal. The portal of the Muqarnas dome occupies the entire length of the façade. The façade is emphasized vertically by the height of the outer walls and the placement of the windows. His four iwan arrangements of madrasas occupy the center of the plan, with the washroom in the center of the courtyard and his four madrasas placed in the corners of the plan. Qibla Iwan is much larger than the other three and is also monumental in size and scope. Instead of integrating the pupil cells into a central space, the cells are arranged along the street façade, whose windows are an integral part of the façade's architecture.
