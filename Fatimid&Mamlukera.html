The History and Features Of Fatimid Caliphate

The Fatimids are a shi'a dynasty orginating from North Africa that ruled Egypt from 966A.D to 1171A.D.
Fatimid rivaled the Abbasid dynasty to rule the islamic world , by claiming their rightful legacy to the caliphate based on their direct descent from prophet Muhammed.
The Fatimids first action was to lay the foundation of a new fortified royal city al-Qahira(Cairo).
The Fatimid state was Egypt's golden period in which islamic art was consummate.
Egyptian artists left behind many reliefs and monuments that attest to the great skill and meticulousness in various art forms.
The last Fatimidian caliph was only eighteen when the Seljuk captured Egypt.

The History and Features Of The Ayyubid Era

It was in 1168 when the victorious Shriko entered Cairo and was named governor of Egypt by the sultan of Damascus , Noor el-Din.
Salah el-Din was one of the most famous figures of the medieval history known in Westren history as saladin.
The young general is one of the few commanders in history who are tremendously respected by thier friends and enemies when he took control over Cairo.
The Fatimids remained isolated in their palaces . saladin did not seek revenge , but waited until their caliph died .
Then he expelled the Fatimids out of their palaces and sent them to exile.

The History and Features Of The Mamluk Era

A succession of strong Mamluk sultans , originally Mamluk slaves based on barracks in Rhoda island and hence named Bahri who took over control of Egypt and Syria from 1250A.D to 1382A.D.
Their reign was characterized by relative stability and prosperity on the internal arena and powerful military.
The turbulent Circassian Mamluk regime that took over the Bahri Mamluk from 1382A.D to 1517A.D was also known as Burgi Mamluk since they were based in the towers of the citadel.
The reign was characterized by epidemic outbreaks , heavy taxation to make up for the decline in revenues that followed the discovery of a new trade route to India.
