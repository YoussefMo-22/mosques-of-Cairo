Significance of The Mosques

Most religions have a place of worship. For Islam, It is the mosque. A Mosque is the house of Allah s.w.t. where only he is worshipped and glorified through the five daily prayers, supplementary (Sunnah), prayers, reciting the Qur'an, zikr (remembrance of Allah s.w.t.), and many other good deeds. Mosque is the center of a community, and many wonderful pieces of Islamic art have been created. Mosque is the place for social welfare, religious festivals, celebrations such as Ramadan, and community work.
Military and political importance: The first Mosque of the Prophet Mohamed had a political office for serving a combined function as a socio-religious and socio-political, he sent the army for Jihad from the mosque in Al-Madinah. Al-Azhar had a major role in the 1919 revolt against the British.
